Reporting Process

How NCIC Works

Every cybercrime report contributes to intelligence development. Our analytical process helps identify trends, patterns, and connections that may support partner government agencies.

01

Submit a Report

Victims, witnesses, and organizations submit cybercrime reports through the appropriate reporting form.

02

Information Analysis

Submitted information is reviewed and analyzed to identify indicators, entities, and relationships.

03

Intelligence Development

Information is transformed into actionable intelligence products through structured analysis.

04

Support Investigations

Relevant intelligence may be shared with partner government agencies to support lawful investigations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

What happens after I submit a report?

Submitted information is reviewed and analyzed to support intelligence development and may assist partner government agencies where appropriate.

Is there a cost to submit a report?

No. Submitting a report through the NCIC reporting portal is free.

What evidence should I preserve?

Preserve emails, screenshots, chat messages, transaction records, wallet addresses, receipts, and any relevant communications.

Can I update my report later?

Instructions for providing additional information will be included with your report confirmation.

Where can I learn more?

Visit the About page, FAQ, or the relevant investigation page for additional information.
